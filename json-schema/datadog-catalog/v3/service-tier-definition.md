Service tiers define the expected reliability, performance, and operational standards for each service in the platform. They help teams make consistent decisions about monitoring, incident response, and prioritisation based on a service's business impact. Every service catalogued in Datadog should be assigned a tier.

> Source: [Service Tier Definition](https://app.notion.com/p/ageras/Service-Tier-Definition-173f148b3ab7815a89e1d487df204ca7)

| Service Tier                           | `spec.tier` | Business Importance                                                                          | Target Uptime                              | Responsiveness                      | Failure Rates                                                    | Monitoring Requirements                                                            | Incident Response Time                 |
| -------------------------------------- | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **Tier 1 — Critical**                  | `"1"`       | Critical to core business operations, revenue generation, or customer experience             | 99.95% (less than 5 minutes/week downtime) | Below 350ms for 95% of requests     | Below 0.05% with robust fault tolerance, monitoring and alerting | Real-time monitoring with proactive alerting for critical metrics                  | Immediate response within 30 minutes   |
| **Tier 2 — Standard**                  | `"2"`       | Supports important business functions with moderate impact on revenue or customer experience | 99.5% (less than 4.5 hours/month downtime) | Below 500ms for 95% of requests     | Below 1% with proactive monitoring and regular maintenance       | Regular monitoring of key metrics with alerting for anomalies                      | Urgent response within 3 hours         |
| **Tier 3 — Non-Critical**              | `"3"`       | Minimal impact on revenue or customer experience; non-essential for day-to-day operations    | 99% (less than 7.2 hours/month downtime)   | Within 1 second for 95% of requests | Tolerable rates with routine maintenance and reactive monitoring | Periodic monitoring of basic health metrics with limited alerting for major issues | Prompt response within 3 business days |
| **None — Experimental / Unmaintained** | `"none"`    | Not yet clarified or actively maintained                                                     | None                                       | Undefined                           | Undefined                                                        | None                                                                               | Undefined                              |

### None — Experimental / Unmaintained

No service level is defined. Regardless of business importance, there are no guarantees or active effort to maintain **uptime, responsiveness, failure rates, monitoring, or incident response times**. If a service in this tier has real business importance, it should be treated as a high-priority candidate for upgrade, refactoring, or deprecation.
